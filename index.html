Why Is Attack Surface Management Important?

Take SolarWinds attacks where malware is introduced by chains of organizations, routes that are often overlooked and thought to be completely secure.

Ransomware, as shown by the recent attack on the Colonial Pipeline, is another example. Attacks target remote services such as Citrix, Remote Desktop Web (RDWeb), or remote desktop protocol (RDP) to gain unauthorized access initially. Because unions are working with remote workers due to the epidemic, time could not have been so bad.

In each of these violations, the attackers entered an unknown route for security reasons or were considered insignificant. Given the large number of devices and services that comprise your business, it is easy to see how something can be overlooked, especially if you are exploring your attack area from a multi-party security perspective - that is, it is internal and external.

The only way to effectively defend against attacks is to take an attack surface management approach that provides the same continuous visibility into your security gaps as attackers have – outside in – so you can remediate issues before they become exploited.
